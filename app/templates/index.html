Fieldston Towers

Welcome to Fieldston Towers, located in the northwestern part of the Bronx. Fieldston Towers is ideal for those seeking the excitement of living in New York City and the comfort of living near nature. Across the street is Van Courtland Park, the third-largest park in NYC, with athletic fields, trails, and public golf courses. To the west is Wave Hill, a public garden with views of the Hudson River and the Palisades.

Fieldston is purely residential with some shops and restaurants a short walk away along Riverdale and Johnson Avenues. It has some of the best public and private schools that beat citywide averages. Transportation to Manhattan is simple, with the number 1 subway line starting at Cortland Park-242nd, several express bus lines, and the Metro-North Railroad trains stopping at the Riverdale section on the Hudson Line.

Fieldston Towers is the place where nature and city life meet. Our community is close to everything, making it convenient for every lifestyle. We offer 1, 2, and 3 bedroom floor plans to meet all of your needs. Call or email us today to check to schedule a tour!

Wave Hill

An urban oasis, Wave Hill’s 28 acres sit high above the Hudson River with sweeping views and lots of open skies--an enchanting surprise for all the senses. Here nature is the springboard for workshops and walks, talks, contemporary art, live performances, and more--there is always something to make, to learn or simply experience, each moment circling back to Wave Hill’s serene setting, inviting us to connect to nature in myriad ways.

Cross County Plaza

This open 240,000 square-foot structure has in excess of 500,000 books and different materials. It reflects and connects with the city's creative energy and goals. A six-story bending, walkable divider grasps the general population court, with shops and administrations at ground level, perusing exhibitions above, and a 300-situate assembly room.

Bronx Zoo

The animals and exhibits at the Bronx Zoo span the globe and include tiny toads from Tanzania and big cats from the Russian Far East. With more than 700 species, the zoo is the perfect place to inspire animal lovers of all ages. Part of our mission is to connect visitors to wildlife and inspire them to join our conservation work.
